What is individual health insurance?

Amid this corona pandemic, people have realized how important it is to keep ourselves safe. And how important it is to keep yourself financially secure. That's why many people are focusing on buying health insurance.

Let us know what is individual health insurance? How does it work? And what are its benefits?

What is individual health insurance?

Individual health insurance is a contract between an insurance provider and the insured. The insurance company pays you the hospital expenses as per the investment made by you in this contract. It is individual health insurance, in which hospitalization expenses are paid to an individual according to the individual sum insured. This insurance is not for all the members of the family but it is for only one person. It has to be bought separately for each person. In most individual health insurance, coverage is provided to an individual for hospitalization expenses, elective treatment, ambulance service, day care treatment, organ donor expenses, etc.

What is covered in individual health insurance?

1.Personal health insurance covers the expenses incurred in the treatment of corona.
2.Pre and post-admission expenses are covered in the hospital.
3.Medical expenses incurred by a person in a hospital for more than 24 hours are covered. This includes room rent, ICU expenses, and medicines.
4.This insurance covers the cost of treatment of Corona.
5.This insurance pays for the treatment at home on the recommendation of the doctor.
6.It also does treatment done through alternative means like Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Yoga, etc.
7.If a person is hospitalized for less than 24 hours, this includes the cost of availing of daycare procedures.
8.It covers the cost of road ambulance services in case of an emergency.
9.If you need an organ transplant, it covers the cost of harvesting a particular organ from a donor.

What is not covered in individual health insurance?

1.Dental treatment, cosmetic treatment, HIV AIDS, obesity, and infertility treatment are not covered.
2.Coverage is not provided for damage or injury caused by alcohol or drug use.
3.Injuries caused by self-harm are not covered.
4.Coverage is not provided for congenital or congenital diseases.
5.Injuries sustained while participating in dangerous sports are not covered.
6.Damages caused by violation of the law or criminal activities are not covered.
7.Injury or illness caused by war, aggression, or nuclear hazards is not covered.

Features and benefits of individual health insurance

Coverage for medical expenses
Health insurance protects you from medical expenses. If you are also facing any disease or health problem then it saves you from the financial crisis. It reduces the burden of your medical expenses. So that you can focus on your health.
Daily allowance for hospitalization Many insurance companies pay a fixed allowance to the insured based on each day spent after hospitalization. This allowance is available to the insured when the insured is hospitalized for several days. The number of these recruitment days is pre-determined in the insurance. Different insurance companies may have different numbers of days.
Add on cover
You can buy optional add-ons to enhance your protection with your insurance policy.
Single coverage
This insurance ensures that only the insured can use the sum assured.
More than one claim
You can make multiple claims in a year, till the sum assured is not exhausted.
Tax benefit or exemption
It helps in saving tax on the premium paid by you under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act.
Insured amount
According to the policy of different insurance companies, it is up to a maximum sum insured of one crore.

What are the things to keep in mind while buying individual health insurance?
There are several things you should keep in mind while buying individual health insurance. Let us know in detail what things should be kept in mind while taking an individual health insurance policy?
1. Policy Expenses
While buying individual health insurance, keep in mind that the policy is available to you at an affordable premium. Choose an insurance policy that is in your budget. One should not buy expensive insurance policies to get more coverage. This may increase your financial burden.
2. Analyze Your Health
You must do your health analysis before buying individual health insurance. After that, make a list of the benefits and coverage that you want under that health insurance. Also, choose the right insurance and estimate your coverage amount.
3. Check the insurance sub-limits
Most health insurance comes with a sub-limit for certain covers. Before choosing the right insurance plan, you should check the sub-limits that come with multiple covers.
4. Check Network Hospital You must check the network hospital of the insurance company while choosing or buying health insurance for yourself. Keep in mind that the network hospital should be near your home or office. If not, then buy the plan from another insurance company.
5. Check Claim Process or Claim Settlement Process
While buying personal health insurance for yourself, you must check the claim process and claim settlement process of the insurance company. Make sure that the claim process and claim settlement process is easy. Also, keep in mind the time taken by the insurance company to process the claim. Don't opt ​​for a plan for a lengthy claim process.
