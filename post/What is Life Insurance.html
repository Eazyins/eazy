What is Life Insurance?

What is life insurance, how does it works, what types of life insurance, benefits of life insurance, what is life insurance

In today's run-of-the-mill life, when do not know what will happen to anyone? In such a situation, if there is only one earning person in a person's house and that person has an accident and dies, then their family has to face financial problems.

Life insurance comes in handy to avoid such situations. Due to this, any person gets financial help after that time is extended to his family.

Let us know in this article what is life insurance? How does life insurance work? What are the benefits of life insurance? And how can it work in your life?

WHAT IS LIFE INSURANCE?

Life insurance is a contract between the person insured and the insurer. In which the person insured has to deposit a fixed amount of premium to the insurance company in a specified period (like 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, or 1 year, etc.).

Under this insurance, the person who is insured at any point in time has an accident and dies, then the insurance company pays a lump sum amount to the insured person or the family of the insured. Life insurance works as financial aid. Which provides financial assistance to the people dependent on you. Life insurance works as a financial backup plan for your family. It helps you to be financially prepared in case any mishap happens to you.

How does life insurance work?

There are many types of life insurance policies. And any person can choose a life insurance policy as per his requirement. And you should take a life insurance policy that suits you.

Life insurance is a contract between the person insured and the insurance company. In which you can choose the right plan of insurance according to you. After choosing the insurance, you get a fixed cover for a specified period. According to that cover, you have to pay a premium to the insurance company. You have to pay this premium on 1 month, 3-month, 6-month, or 1-year basis. After that the insured person i.e. the insured dies due for any reason, then a lump sum amount is paid by the insurance company to the family member of the insured person in the form of a sum assured i.e. insurance cover. This amount can be anything as per your insurance. The beneficiary can use this amount in any way.

Types of life insurance

There are 8 types of life insurance in India. The insured can choose his insurance according to his need. Let us know how many types of life insurance are there and what are their names?
Term life insurance
Term Life Insurance It is a type of life insurance that allows the insured to pay a fixed sum of money as a death benefit on the death of the insured during a specified period. Term life insurance is also known as simple and pure life insurance. Once its term is over, the policyholder can either extend it for another term or allow the life insurance policy to lapse.
Endowment policy
An endowment policy is a traditional life insurance policy that helps you to save along with insurance. It fulfills your twin goals. This plan helps the insured to make regular savings for a particular period. And on completion of its period, a lump sum amount is paid to the insured. If the insured survives for a specified period, then the full maturity amount is paid to him. And if the insured person dies, the bonus (if any) and the sum assured are paid to the insured's family or nominee.
Unit linked insurance
Unit Linked Insurance Plan is such an insurance plan in which both investment and insurance facilities are available. Under this insurance, a part of the premium paid by this person is used for insurance while the other part is invested. In this, depending on the risk-taking of the person insured, you can take this insurance according to the plan given by different companies. In this, the money deposited by the insurance company is used to buy shares and equities.

Whole life insurance policy
A whole life insurance plan is permanent life insurance. That is, this life insurance gives the facility to ensure the whole life of the person. Or in some cases till the age of 100 years. While buying this type of life insurance, a fixed amount is fixed, which is called Sum Assured. After that, the insured has to pay the premium according to that sum assured While buying this type of life insurance, a fixed amount is fixed, which is called Sum Assured. After that, the insured has to pay the premium according to that sum assured. After that person dies in any way, then the nominee of that insured is paid the sum assured as decided by the insurance company.
Money-back plan
In money back, life insurance, in addition to the lump sum assured, the insured gets some part of the sum assured at regular intervals. If the insured dies, the nominee is paid the entire sum assured plus a bonus (if any). This insurance is suitable for risk-averse individuals. In this type of insurance policy, the insured can also save. In this way, the insured can fulfill his short-term financial objective.
Child insurance policy
This insurance plan is specially designed for children. This insurance plan comes in handy for the future development of the children and their better future. This insurance provides the benefit of financial assistance for children's marriage, education, and other expenses. It is also known as an investment and savings policy. It secures the future of the children financially. In case of unfortunate death of the insured, a lump sum amount is paid to the family of the insured. But even after this, insurance does not end. And all future premiums of the insurance are waived off. The insurance company continues to pay the premium or investment on behalf of the insured and the insurance continues for its stipulated time.
Pension and Retirement Insurance
This plan helps you to financially support your post-retirement life. It is a retirement or pension plan, you do not get life insurance cover under this plan. To plan your retirement, there are many pension plans available in the market, these plans vary from company to company. By choosing the right plan, you can build your retirement corpus by assessing your risk. After this, after the specified period according to your plan, you are paid a certain amount as a pension. This payout amount can be one month, three months, six months, or years depending on your plan.
Investment and Savings Insurance Scheme
This type of insurance plan helps you to save and invest. If you want to save, then you can invest some money in your daily expenses and invest in this type of insurance plan. This plan can help you save money to meet your short-term and long-term financial goals. This plan is a mix of traditional and linked schemes. This insurance also pays a fixed amount to your family after the death of the insured.

Benefits of life insurance

Financial Security: Life insurance helps in giving financial security to the family if there is only one breadwinner in a household and he dies due to some reason. So that their family can pay for their children's education and the debts of debtors.
Investment: A life insurance plan can give you better returns as compared to other investment options. Many life insurance plans offer bonuses, which no other investment plan offers. The money invested in life insurance gives good returns and the money is returned after the death of the insured. The money invested in it remains safe.
Build Wealth: Many life insurance plans allow you to build wealth. In addition to life cover, many life insurance plans offer an opportunity to invest in many stocks and equities.
Life insurance can come in handy for a person's final expenses: In a family, there is only one earning person and when he dies, sometimes their relatives or family members do not have money. Due to this, they do not have money for their funeral and social customs after their death. In such a situation, if the deceased person has any insurance, then it can be useful in the last moment of his family members.
Tax benefit or benefit: The amount paid for the purchase of life insurance i.e. premium is eligible for deduction of tax under section 80C of the Income Tax Act. Presently the amount available for exemption under section 80C is Rs 1,00,000 which can be invested in life insurance premium, retirement pension, equity-linked mutual fund scheme, and public provident fund (maximum Rs 70,000).
Buy at a young age and save more: A life insurance plan gives you good cover at low premium rates if you buy it at a young age. If you buy the same insurance policy when you are older, you will be paying a much higher premium than that plan.
Loan Option: Life insurance gives you the advantage of taking a policy loan when you need money. The loan amount can be taken either from the Cash Value or part of the Sum Assured under the policy, depending on the scheme provisions.
Help to meet the goal: Life insurance helps you to plan for the life stage. Where you can plan your life financial goals at your convenience. Life insurance helps you plan according to your needs.
