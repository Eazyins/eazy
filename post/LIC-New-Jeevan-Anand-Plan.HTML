LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan

What is LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan, Eligibility, Features, Benefits, Surrender Value, Riders, Grace Period

LIC New Jeevan Anand is a life insurance plan. This insurance is a combination of savings and protection. In which the benefit of maturity is available at the end of the policy term and the coverage of sum assured for a lifetime is available like term insurance. This insurance policy is highly beneficial for those who want to earn huge returns while they are alive.

What is LIC New Jeevan Anand's Plan?

LIC New Jeevan Anand is a participating, individual, and non-linked life insurance plan. It offers a combination of savings and security. The plan provides financial protection against death throughout the life of the policyholder with the provision of a lump sum payment at the end of the selected policy term in case the policyholder survives.
This policy also takes care of the requirements through its loan facility. This is one of the most traditionally available endowment plans from LIC. This is one of the best-selling endowment plans by LIC. Thus it is an endowment as well as a whole life insurance plan.

LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan Eligibility
Insurance Name:- LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan (Plan No.915)
Age of Entry:- Minimum:- 18 days
Maximum:- 50 years
Coverage (Basic Sum Assured):- Minimum:- Rs. 1,00,000
Maximum:- No Limit (As per Board Approval)
Premium Payment Mode:- Monthly, Quarterly, Half Yearly, Yearly
Policy Term:- Minimum: 15 Years
Maximum: 35 Years
Maturity Age:- Maximum: 75 Years

Features of LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan

1.LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan is a traditional non-linked and participating, individual whole life endowment plan.
2.In case of unfortunate death of the insured, the death benefit is provided to the beneficiary of the insured.
3.The premiums in this plan can be paid at regular intervals like yearly, half-yearly, quarterly or monthly, etc.
4.A loan facility is available to the insured under this plan. Provided that at least 2 full years' premiums have been paid.
5.Disability and Accidental Death Benefit Rider is also available in this plan of LIC on payment of an additional premium amount.
6.Avail tax-saving benefits on premiums paid and claim amount (maturity and death benefits) under Section 80C and Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, 1961.
7.Switching of this policy is not allowed.

Benefits of LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan

Death Benefit The following death benefit will be paid on payment of all due premiums:
On death during the policy term i.e. before the stipulated date of maturity, Simple Reversionary Bonus and Final Additional Bonus (if any) equal to the Sum Assured will be paid.
In case of death of the insured, the beneficiary of the insured will receive the following sum assured amount, which is as follows:
125% of Basic Sum Assured
7 times the annual premium
No less than 105 percent of the total premiums are paid till the date of death.
The above premium rates are excluding tax, extra premium, and rider premium (if any).
The Basic Sum Assured will be payable on death after the end of the policy term i.e. from the stipulated date of maturity.
Maturity Benefit Sum Assured Final Bonus on Maturity and Simple Reversionary Bonus will be given on survival of the Life Assured till the date of maturity provided the policy is active i.e. all future due premiums have been paid. Wherein the Sum Assured on Maturity is equal to the Basic Basic Sum Assured.
Profit sharing LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan will participate in Company's profits and will be eligible to receive a Simple Reversionary Bonus declared as per Company's experience during the policy term provided the policy is an inactive process.
Tax benefits Avail tax-saving benefits on premiums paid and claim amount (maturity and death benefits) under Section 80C and Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, 1961.
Riders of LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan LIC Plan 915 offers the following four options for the riders which can be opted for by paying an additional premium.
LIC Accident Benefit Rider
LIC Accidental Death & Disability Benefit Rider
LIC New Term Assurance Rider
LIC New Critical Illness Rider
Grace period
There is a grace period of 30 days for quarterly, half-yearly, and annual premium payments. Same if you pay a monthly premium then you get 15 days off. If the premium is not paid before the expiry date of the grace period, the policy will terminate.
Surrender benefit This insurance plan provides an option to surrender the policy at any time after the policy has been in force for two consecutive years. In such situations, the insurer pays the higher special surrender value or the surrender value equal to the guaranteed surrender value.
Revival If the premium is not paid within the grace period, the policy terminates. LIC New Jeevan Anand Plan offers the option to revive the policy within 5 consecutive years. This means from the date of your first unpaid premium and it can be revived only before the maturity date.
Freelook period If the insured is not satisfied with the terms and conditions of the policy, it can be returned to the insurance company within 15 days from the date of receipt of the policy documents stating the reasons for the objection.
