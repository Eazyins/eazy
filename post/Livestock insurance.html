Livestock insurance

Information related to Livestock Insurance Scheme

Animal Insurance Scheme is a scheme implemented by the Central Government. Under which some selected districts of the country have been included in this scheme and this scheme is also providing benefits to the cattle farmers living in 300 districts of the country. The main objective of starting the scheme has been made to save the farmers from the loss caused to their livestock owners due to the death of their animals due to any reason.

Along with this, it has been done to inform the people about the benefits of livestock insurance and to popularize them with the goal of quality development of livestock products. If you also keep animals and you want to get your animals insured, then here we will tell you what is the animal insurance scheme, Livestock Insurance Scheme in Hindi, online registration process, all the information is given in this post.

What is Animal Insurance

The main objective of the animal insurance scheme is to provide protection to those animal owners from their losses, which farmers make their living by rearing animals. A pastoralist suffers a greater loss when an animal dies due to the occurrence of some natural event, due to some disease, or accident. Keeping this in mind, this scheme has been started.

Indigenous/hybrid milch cattle have been kept under this scheme. These animals are insured on the basis of their prevailing prices in the market. Installment of insurance is allowed up to 50% only. The entire cost is borne by the Central Government. The benefit of the scheme grant will be available to only two animals per beneficiary with a policy of three years.

Selection of Related Animals and Beneficiaries in Animal Insurance Scheme

Animals like indigenous/hybrid milch cattle and buffaloes have been kept in the scheme, in addition to buffalo milking and non-milk animals, in addition to pregnant cattle which have given birth to a calf, have been included.

Those animals who are already beneficiary of any other scheme implemented by the government, they will be deprived of the benefit of this scheme.

The benefit of the scheme is only for the cattle owners having two animals and the insurance of one animal can be up to a maximum of 3 years.

Farmers will be encouraged to take a three-year policy in case of natural calamities like drought and floods. So that they can get the real benefit of insurance, apart from this, if a farmer wants to take a policy of less than three years, then he can also take it, and if the insurance is insured next year, a grant will also be available in the insurance installment.

The insurance of the animal will be based on the highest price it can get in the market. Which will be included by the beneficiary, authorized vet and insurance agent.

On the death of the cattle covered under the scheme, the amount of the scheme will be provided by the insurance company to the animal owner.

The amount of compensation under the scheme will be given to the animal husbandry within 15 days of the death of the animal.

Due to this scheme, if the animal dies, the animal owner will not have to suffer much loss.

Compensation will be paid on the current market value of the animal.

Identification of the Insured Animal

The animals that are insured under the scheme are given a separate identity so that they can be easily identified while claiming the sum insured. For this identification, the ears of the animals are marked in a unique way, which should be kept safe by all means. At the time of taking the policy, ear marking or recent microchip is applied.

The cost of putting this identification mark is borne by the insurance company and the responsibility of maintenance will be of the animal owners who take the benefit. The marking done in the ear of the cattle and the choice of material used is on the consent of both the insurance company and the beneficiary.
